The Self-Custody Advantage

Why does self-custody matter for spending? Consider what happens when an exchange fails:

  • FTX users lost access to their funds for years
  • Celsius froze withdrawals with no warning
  • BlockFi went bankrupt owing billions

With a self-custody card, your funds stay in your wallet until the moment of purchase. No exchange can freeze them. No company failure can lock you out. You maintain sovereignty over your money.

How Onchain Cards Work

The technical flow varies by provider, but generally:

  1. You connect your wallet (or a dedicated spending wallet) to the card provider
  2. When you swipe/tap, the provider checks your balance
  3. You approve the transaction in your wallet (some cards use pre-authorized limits)
  4. The provider converts your crypto to fiat and settles with the merchant
  5. The crypto leaves your wallet only after confirmation

This differs from custodial cards where you deposit funds to the provider first.
